  • 02

    Max-Q

    The Maximum aerodynamic pressure is reached and aerodynamic stresses are lifted.

    Max-Q is the point where the largest amount of aerodynamic stress is exerted on the vehicle, which is just a way of saying the rocket is feeling a lot of physical pressure from shoving its way through all the air in the atmosphere at a really high speed. In fact, it’s going through the most physical stress it will feel during the entire launch.

    From t+1,14 to t+2,25

    Speed: 1500 to 6000 km/h

    Altitude: 14 to 70 km

    Main Engine Cut-Off

    At this point we cut the engines of the first stage in order to limit the acceleration because the thrust of the engines of this stage is not adjustable.

    The engine of our Falcon 9 is the powerful Merlin 1D that benefits a ground thrust of 654kN and a scalable boost from 70 to 100.

    The engines lights up again just after the separation which is our next step.

    Speed: 6000 km/h

    Altitude: 70 to 80 km

    Separation

    Here happen the Separation of the first stage with the second stage at relatively low speed, so its reentry starts off drastically slower than a reentry from orbit, we are using pneumatic pushers instead of being assisted with rockets, while the second stage follow his own trajectory.

    Altitude: 80 km
